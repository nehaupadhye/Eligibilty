Eligibilty Requirements

Any healthy adult, both male and female, can donate blood. Men can donate safely once in every 3 months while women can donate every 4 months

Weight

Donor should be more than 45 kilograms

Age

Donor should be in the age group of 18 to 65

Heart Disease

Donor should not have any heart diseases

Child Birth

should have delivered 1 year ago and stopped lactation

Diabetes

Donor should not be treated with insulin and injections

Haemoglobin

Haemoglobin should be more than 12.5 grams

Pulse

Temperature and Pulse should be normal

Infection

Donor should not have Hepatitis B, C, Tuberculosis, Leprosy, HIV

Blood Pressure

The systolic and diastolic blood pressures are within normal limits

Cancer

Should not have any forms of cancer

Drug

You cannot give blood if injected intravenously, even once

Malaria

Malaria should not have been treated for malaria in last 3 months or 3 years if residing in endemic areas

Surgery

Donor should have undergone any kind of surgery

Immunization and Vaccination

Should not have had shots for- Cholera, Typhoid, Diptheria, Tetanus, Plague in last 15 days and Rabies vaccination in last one year

Medication

Certain medication may be risky to donor or recipient and if you're taking them you will not be able to give blood
